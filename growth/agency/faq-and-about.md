## About / approach

**H2:** We're developers who run AI in production. So we can get your team there too.

tsukumo is a developer studio and AI consultancy, one team, one story. We build our own
products by running fleets of agents in production (the open suite WRAI.TH, trovex, and yoru
is the proof), and we transition client teams to work the same way.

That order matters. We didn't start as consultants who read about agents. We're builders who
hit every wall, context, cost, reliability, coordination, getting agents to do real work, and
built the layers that solve them. When we help your team, we're installing an operating model
we run ourselves, not improvising on your budget.

**Our approach, in three commitments:**

- **We augment your developers; we never replace them.** Your devs become the operators who
  run the agents. The craft stays theirs, the output gets much bigger. This is the honest
  position and the one that actually works.
- **We work in production, on your standards.** Your stack, your CI, your review culture, your
  constraints. No POC theater, no greenfield fantasy, no rip-and-replace.
- **We leave you independent.** The goal is your team operating agents without us. We train
  the capability in, then step back. A dependency on tsukumo is a failure, not a business
  model.

If that sounds like the opposite of "buy a tool and hope," it is.

## FAQ

### Isn't AI easy now? We already have the seats.

Seats give your team a copilot, autocomplete and Q&A in the editor. That's real, and it's
about 10% of what these models can do. The other 90%, agents running real work reliably in
production, is an operating problem (context, orchestration, observability, trained
operators), and none of it comes in the license. The "AI is easy" feeling is exactly the gap
between a demo and production. If it were easy, your team would already be there.

### Isn't the point of AI to ship cheaper?

No, and if cheaper is the goal we're not the right fit. The point is prod-grade output and
roughly 10x from the team you already trust. We're not here to shrink your team or your
budget; we're here to make a good team dramatically more capable. Teams that chase "cheaper"
tend to ship more mediocre work faster, which is not the same thing.

### Will you replace our developers?

No. Our entire model is the opposite: your developers become the agentic operators. We
augment them. A good developer running good agents beats either one alone, and a team that
fears being replaced will never push the tools to their limit. We make your devs stronger and
far more productive, not redundant.

### Why you, and not a freelancer or a big consultancy?

A freelancer can write you some prompts; they rarely run agent fleets in production or
transfer a durable capability to your whole team. A big consultancy will sell you a
slide-heavy "AI transformation" by people who haven't shipped it. We're a small studio of
developers who run this in production on our own products. You get builders who've done the
thing, working on your real codebase, leaving your team able to keep going without us.

### Do you respect our existing environment, or do we start over?

We respect it. We transition the team and stack you have, inside your conventions and
controls. The whole point is to upgrade how your existing system works, not to replace it
with ours.

### What do we actually walk away with?

A team that operates agents in production, the workflows and supporting layers running on your
repo, and developers trained to keep extending them. Where we build product (the studio side),
you also walk away with the shipped software.

### How do you price this?

Around the capability we transfer, not seats or demo hours. Specifics depend on scope; we set
them with you in scoping. *(No placeholder numbers here, we quote real figures in the
proposal, never invented ones.)*

### Is this just for big companies?

No, it's aimed at startups and scale-ups whose teams use AI as a copilot and can feel a bigger
capability they haven't reached. If your CTO has watched AI demos dazzle and then die in your
own environment, that's exactly who we work with.
