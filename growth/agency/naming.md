# Agency naming, candidates for the fleet-ops consulting firm

*Owner: content-lead · candidates only. **Owner decides the final name**, this is a curated
shortlist for selection, not a pick.*

**What we're naming:** the consulting business the OSS suite funnels into. It helps teams run
**fleets of AI coding agents** well at scale (cost, consistency, one source of truth, the ops
of agentic dev). The OSS tools (WRAI.TH, trovex, yoru) are the proof of competence and the
top of the funnel; this firm is where the money is.

**House aesthetic to play with or against:** WRAI.TH (wraith), yoru (夜, "night"), mystical,
JP-tinged, night/shadow, terminal-native. Brief asks for some names that fit the house and
some that deliberately don't.

**Constraints honored:** no AI-slop, no corporate-consulting cliché (no Nexus/Catalyst/
Synergy/Elevate/Quantum/Pinnacle), dev-credible, sayable, terminal-friendly wordmark.

**Domains:** whois/DNS lookup was unavailable in this environment, so every domain below is
marked **VERIFY**, do not treat any as confirmed available. Check `.com`/`.dev`/`.sh` (and
`.io`/`.ai`) with a registrar before committing. Patterns suggested per name.

---

## Direction A, house-fit (mystical / JP-tinged / night / terminal)

These extend the WRAI.TH/yoru world: unseen hands that keep the fleet running in the dark.

### 1. Tsukumo
- **Rationale:** from 付喪神 *tsukumogami*, tools that gain a spirit after long use. The exact
  metaphor for coding agents: ordinary tools that come alive. Deep house-fit with a real
  meaning, not just a vibe.
- **Vibe:** mythic, precise, a little uncanny. Pairs naturally with wraith and yoru.
- **Positioning:** "We help your tools come alive without coming apart, fleet-ops for teams
  running AI coding agents."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** tsukumo.dev / tsukumo.sh / tsukumo.com (likely contested, common JP word)

### 2. Onibi
- **Rationale:** 鬼火 *onibi*, the ghost-fire / will-o'-the-wisp that flickers at night. Reads
  as the small lights that guide a fleet through the dark.
- **Vibe:** eerie, guiding, nocturnal. Strong house-fit.
- **Positioning:** "The lights that keep your agent fleet on course."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** onibi.dev / onibi.sh / onibi.ai

### 3. Wisp
- **Rationale:** will-o'-the-wisp in plain English, an unseen guide. Accessible version of the
  Onibi idea; pairs with wraith without needing JP literacy.
- **Vibe:** light, quiet, a touch spectral. House-adjacent, more approachable.
- **Positioning:** "Quiet guidance for teams running agents at scale."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** wisp.sh / wisp.dev (short English word, .com almost certainly taken)

### 4. Kuro
- **Rationale:** 黒 *kuro*, black / the dark. Short, hard, terminal-native wordmark; sits in
  the night-house with wraith and yoru.
- **Vibe:** minimal, sharp, monochrome-terminal.
- **Positioning:** "Run your agent fleet in the dark and trust it anyway."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** kuro.sh / kuro.dev (short, contested)

### 5. Revenant
- **Rationale:** a spirit that returns to set things right, the firm that comes in and makes a
  messy agent rollout work. Direct sibling to "wraith."
- **Vibe:** dramatic, gothic, memorable (maybe a touch dark for some buyers).
- **Positioning:** "We bring order back to teams drowning in their own agents."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** revenant.dev / revenant.sh / revenant.ops

## Direction B, fleet-ops / operator (dev-credible, what they actually do)

Old-craft and nautical operator metaphors. Reads as competence and hands-on work, not magic.

### 6. Drydock
- **Rationale:** where a fleet is refitted and kept seaworthy. Concrete, ops-flavored, instantly
  legible: you bring your fleet in, it leaves running right.
- **Vibe:** industrial, capable, no-nonsense.
- **Positioning:** "Where teams bring their agent fleets to run right."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** drydock.dev / drydock.sh (drydock.com likely taken)

### 7. Quartermaster
- **Rationale:** the officer who keeps a fleet provisioned and moving. Exactly the job: keep the
  agents supplied with the right context and running.
- **Vibe:** authoritative, logistical, classic.
- **Positioning:** "Provisioning and ops for fleets of coding agents."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** quartermaster.dev / qm.sh (long word, abbreviation may be needed)

### 8. Bosun
- **Rationale:** the boatswain, the hands-on one who keeps the crew and gear working. Short,
  sayable, less grand than Quartermaster, more "in the trenches."
- **Vibe:** practical, salty, terminal-short.
- **Positioning:** "We keep your agent crew working."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** bosun.sh / bosun.dev

### 9. Fleetwright
- **Rationale:** -wright = maker/fixer (shipwright, wheelwright). "We build and keep your fleet."
  Old-craft credibility, and "fleet" says the category out loud.
- **Vibe:** craftsmanlike, sturdy, honest.
- **Positioning:** "Built and kept right: fleet-ops for agentic teams."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** fleetwright.com / fleetwright.dev

## Direction C, plain dev-honest (no mysticism, says what it is)

For an owner who wants straightforward over atmospheric.

### 10. Cold Start
- **Rationale:** every dev knows the cold-start tax; this firm kills it (agents re-deriving from
  zero each session). Ironic, memorable, terminal-native.
- **Vibe:** wry, technical, in-jokey.
- **Positioning:** "Stop paying the cold-start tax on your agents."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** coldstart.dev / coldstart.sh (coldstart.com likely taken)

### 11. Agentwright
- **Rationale:** the -wright craft idea, made literal: the people who get your agents right.
  On-the-nose but unambiguous and category-claiming.
- **Vibe:** plain, credible, slightly utilitarian.
- **Positioning:** "We get your team's coding agents right."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** agentwright.com / agentwright.dev

### 12. Throughline
- **Rationale:** the single consistent line through a team's agents and docs, the SSOT idea as
  a firm name. Calm, non-technical, but meaningful.
- **Vibe:** considered, editorial, grown-up.
- **Positioning:** "One throughline for every agent on your team."
- **Domains (VERIFY):** throughline.dev (throughline.com likely taken, established word)

## content-lead's read (for curation, not a decision)

- **Strongest house-fit + meaning:** **Tsukumo** (tools that gain a spirit = AI agents, the
  metaphor is unusually exact) and **Onibi** (guiding night-fire). Both deepen the WRAI.TH/yoru
  world.
- **Most dev-credible / legible:** **Drydock** and **Fleetwright**, a buyer instantly gets the
  job from the name.
- **Best wildcard:** **Cold Start**, high recall with devs, says the value, terminal-native.

**Watch-outs:** Revenant may read too dark for some enterprise buyers; Quartermaster/
Throughline are long; common-word names (Wisp, Kuro, Tsukumo) will have contested `.com`s, so
plan around `.dev`/`.sh`. Verify trademarks too, not just domains.

Owner picks; happy to expand any direction or run a second round once a direction is chosen.
