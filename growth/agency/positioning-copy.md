## The gap (the thing CTOs have felt)

You bought the seats. Your devs have Claude Code. It autocompletes, it answers, it's a better
search. And you can tell that's maybe 10% of what this is, because you keep seeing demos of AI
doing far more, and none of it survives contact with your real codebase.

That gap, between a copilot in the editor and agents that actually run work in production, is
where most teams are stuck. It isn't a license problem or a model problem. It's an operating
problem: how do you make agents reliable, cheap, and trustworthy on a real codebase with real
standards, without breaking what works or scaring the people who built it.

That gap is what we do.

## What tsukumo does

Two doors into the same capability.

**Studio, we build it.** Product and AI systems, shipped to production at studio pace. The
software we ship for ourselves (WRAI.TH, trovex, yoru) is the proof: we run our own agent
fleets to build them. You get a product and a demonstration of the way of working.

**Consulting, we transition your team.** We take a team that uses AI as a copilot and turn it
into a team that runs agents as operators, in production. On your existing environment, your
standards, your stack. We augment your devs; we do not replace them. The craft stays theirs,
and the output multiplies.

## How we work (the part that's different)

- **In production, not in a deck.** No POC theater. The goal is agents doing real work on your
  real repo, measured, from early on.
- **Augment, never replace.** Your devs become the operators. This kills the fear that makes
  AI rollouts fail, and it's the honest position: good devs running good agents beat either
  one alone.
- **Respect the environment.** We work inside your conventions, CI, and review culture, not a
  greenfield fantasy. Transition, not rip-and-replace.
- **We build, so we know.** We're developers who ship AI products. We've felt every failure
  mode we help you avoid.

## Who this is for

CTOs at startups and scale-ups who've felt the gap, who want their team shipping at a level
seats alone won't reach, and who respect their developers enough to want them stronger, not
sidelined.

## Who this is not for

If you think AI is easy, that buying licenses is the same as capability, or that the point of
AI is to ship the same thing cheaper, we're not your studio. Tools and seats are not
prod-grade AI capability. This isn't about cheaper. It's about prod-grade and roughly 10x the
output from the team you already trust.
