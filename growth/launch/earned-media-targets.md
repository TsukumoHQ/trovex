## 0. The honest framing (every pitch obeys this)

- **Give editorial value, don't pitch a product.** "Here's what coding agents waste on context, with real data" — trovex is the footnote that proves you've done it.
- **Match the angle to their audience** (column in each table). A wrong-fit pitch reads as spam.
- **One reproducible number** (~60%, repo-dependent) — never invented, always verifiable.
- **No company name; founder voice ("I").** lowercase `trovex`. No banned words.
- **Stay on the EDITORIAL track** — several outlets have a parallel *paid/sponsor* track; do not confuse the two (flagged below).

**A. Contributed-article pitch (InfoQ / The New Stack):**
```
Subject: Contributed article pitch — context engineering for coding agents (cutting token overhead at scale)

Hi [editor],

[One specific line on a recent piece of theirs it follows on from.]

I'd like to contribute a non-promotional, technical piece for [outlet]'s readers on a problem that's
getting expensive fast: coding agents reread a repo's docs every session to find the canonical one,
and the redundant context dominates the token bill. I'd cover how to measure it, the patterns that cut
it (route to one current doc + freshness + section reads instead of full rereads), and the trade-offs.

Working outline:
- the hidden cost of redundant context in agentic dev workflows
- measuring it (a simple before/after on a real repo)
- patterns that reduce it + where they don't help
- what to watch as fleets/teams scale

I write from building an open-source tool in this space, but the piece stands on its own — no product
pitch. Draft in [N] days if it's a fit. Happy to adjust the angle.

[name]
```

[name]
