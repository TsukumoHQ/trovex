## LinkedIn version (founder, build-in-public framing)

A tool that only lists what it's good at is selling you something. Here's what trovex doesn't do — because that's where you decide if it's worth your afternoon.

It indexes your markdown docs, not your source code. It won't search your functions.

The ~60% fewer tokens is the doc-lookup slice, on a doc-heavy repo. Thin docs, smaller win. The number is yours to measure on your own repo, not a figure to take on faith.

It's retrieval, not a model. It serves the one currently-correct doc as a path:line pointer; the agent still reasons. And it runs locally — SQLite, ONNX, no keys, your code stays on the box — which also means no hosted service is doing the work. You run it.

The test is simple: if your coding agents reread the same docs every session, trovex cuts that. If they don't, it won't help, and I'd rather you know before you install.
